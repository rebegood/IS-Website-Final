Leg day

Hitting leg day is awesome because it not only strengthens major muscle groups like the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes but also enhances overall athleticism and stability. Additionally, leg exercises promote better metabolic health by stimulating large muscle groups, aiding in fat loss and improving cardiovascular endurance. Incorporating leg workouts into your routine can also lead to better posture and reduced risk of injury in various physical activities.

  1. Hamstrings and Glutes
    • Sumo Deadlift
    • Forward-leaning dumbbell squat
    • KAS Hip Thrust
    • Barbell Glute Bridge
    • Hamstring curls
  2. Quads and Calves
    • Front squat
    • Bulgarian split squat
    • Close-stance leg press
    • Calf raises

Upper body day

Hitting upper body day is awesome because it targets key muscle groups such as the chest, back, shoulders, and arms, fostering a balanced physique and improving overall strength. Additionally, upper body workouts contribute to functional fitness, enhancing daily activities like lifting, pushing, and pulling. Strengthening the upper body not only enhances aesthetic appeal but also boosts confidence and promotes better posture, leading to improved overall well-being.

  1. Push
    • Deficit push ups
    • Arnold presses
    • Zottman curls
    • Lateral Raises/Front Raises superset
    • Cable tricep extensions
  2. Pull
    • Close-grip assisted pull ups
    • Single arm dumbbell rows
    • Cable rows
    • Face pulls
